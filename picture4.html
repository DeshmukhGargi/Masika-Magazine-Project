Rise of Graphic Novels in India :

Graphic novels are becoming increasingly popular in India as they combine storytelling with visual art. This format attracts young readers, helps improve visual literacy, and makes complex stories easier to understand.

In recent years, Indian creators have used graphic novels to explore mythology, history, social issues, and personal narratives. Improved publishing platforms and digital access have further contributed to the growth of graphic novels in India.

Reasons for the Growth of Graphic Novels in India :

  • Visual storytelling appeals to young and new readers

  • Combination of art and literature enhances engagement

  • Exploration of Indian mythology and culture

  • Use of graphic novels to discuss social and political issues

  • Growing digital platforms and independent publishers

Popular Indian Graphic Novels :

  • Amar Chitra Katha – Anant Pai

  • Corridor – Sarnath Banerjee

  • Kari – Amruta Patil

  • The Harappa Files – V.G. Suresh

  • Munnu: A Boy from Kashmir – Malik Sajad
